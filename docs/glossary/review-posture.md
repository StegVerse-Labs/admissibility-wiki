# Review Posture

## Equivalent Terms

No accepted equivalent terms are recorded yet.

Equivalent-term claims should be submitted through the proposal lifecycle and accepted only through a decision record.

## Overlapping Terms

The following terms may overlap with review posture, but are not treated as identical without review:

- review status;
- approval status;
- oversight status;
- human review;
- quorum review.

## Adjacent Terms

The following terms are adjacent because they may involve review or oversight without defining the review condition relevant to a transition at commit time:

- approval workflow;
- change approval;
- sign-off;
- audit review;
- escalation path.

## Definition

Review posture describes the state of human, automated, delegated, or quorum-based review for a proposed transition.

It answers whether review is absent, optional, required, completed, blocked, delegated, escalated, or refused.

Review posture is the review condition relevant to a transition at commit time.

## Why The StegVerse Term Is Retained

The StegVerse term is retained because overlapping terms may describe review or approval state without preserving whether that state had standing when consequence was allowed to bind.

## Common Postures

Common postures may include:

- not required;
- required;
- completed;
- pending;
- blocked;
- delegated;
- escalated;
- refused.

## Why It Matters

A transition may have technical validity and evidence while still requiring review before consequence can bind.

Admissibility must preserve whether review was required and whether that requirement was satisfied.
