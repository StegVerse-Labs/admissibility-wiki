# Transition

## Equivalent Terms

No accepted equivalent terms are recorded yet.

Equivalent-term claims should be submitted through the proposal lifecycle and accepted only through a decision record.

## Overlapping Terms

The following terms may overlap with transition, but are not treated as identical without review:

- state change;
- state transition;
- workflow transition;
- transaction;
- lifecycle transition.

## Adjacent Terms

The following terms are adjacent because they may describe nearby system movement without necessarily carrying governance consequence:

- event;
- action;
- operation;
- request;
- process step.

## Definition

A transition is a movement from one governed state to another.

In StegVerse vocabulary, a transition is not merely a technical change. It is a governance-relevant change that may carry consequence.

## Why The StegVerse Term Is Retained

The StegVerse term is retained because overlapping terms may describe movement or state change without requiring authority, policy, evidence, review posture, receipt, and consequence-standing analysis.

## Transition Components

A transition can include:

- an originating state;
- a proposed target state;
- an actor or agent;
- an authority claim;
- a policy reference;
- an evidence posture;
- a review posture;
- a decision result;
- a receipt.

## Transition Question

The core transition question is:

> May this proposed state change bind under the current authority, policy, evidence, and context conditions?
