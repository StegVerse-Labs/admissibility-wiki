# Evidence Posture

## Equivalent Terms

No accepted equivalent terms are recorded yet.

Equivalent-term claims should be submitted through the proposal lifecycle and accepted only through a decision record.

## Overlapping Terms

The following terms may overlap with evidence posture, but are not treated as identical without review:

- evidence state;
- evidence quality;
- evidence sufficiency;
- evidentiary status;
- proof status.

## Adjacent Terms

The following terms are adjacent because they may describe evidence handling without defining the evidence condition used for a transition decision:

- audit evidence;
- supporting documentation;
- artifact availability;
- data quality;
- source reliability.

## Definition

Evidence posture describes the state of the evidence used to evaluate a proposed transition.

It answers whether the available evidence is present, missing, stale, incomplete, conflicting, or sufficient for the transition being reviewed.

Evidence posture is the evidence condition at or near commit time.

## Why The StegVerse Term Is Retained

The StegVerse term is retained because overlapping terms may describe evidence quality or availability without linking that evidence condition to admissibility, authority, policy, review, and commit-time consequence.

## Common Postures

Common postures may include:

- present;
- missing;
- stale;
- incomplete;
- conflicting;
- sufficient;
- insufficient.

## Why It Matters

A transition can have an actor, policy reference, and technical path while still lacking adequate evidence.

Admissibility should not treat missing or stale evidence as equivalent to sufficient evidence.
