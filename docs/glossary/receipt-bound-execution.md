# Receipt-Bound Execution

## Equivalent Terms

No accepted equivalent terms are recorded yet.

Equivalent-term claims should be submitted through the proposal lifecycle and accepted only through a decision record.

## Overlapping Terms

The following terms may overlap with receipt-bound execution, but are not treated as identical without review:

- auditable execution;
- traceable execution;
- logged execution;
- evidence-backed execution;
- provenance-bound operation.

## Adjacent Terms

The following terms are adjacent because they may preserve evidence or history without necessarily binding execution to admissibility posture:

- audit trail;
- activity log;
- event log;
- provenance record;
- signed attestation.

## Definition

Receipt-bound execution is execution connected to a durable record of the authority, policy, evidence, context, and decision posture present at commit time.

The receipt is not merely an activity log. It is governance evidence for why a transition result was accepted, refused, escalated, or rejected.

## Why The StegVerse Term Is Retained

The StegVerse term is retained because overlapping terms may record that execution happened without recording whether the transition had standing to bind consequence at commit time.

## Receipt Purpose

A receipt should help answer:

- what action was proposed;
- who or what proposed it;
- which authority class applied;
- which policy reference applied;
- what evidence was available;
- what decision was made;
- what drift was detected;
- whether the transition result was permitted to bind.
