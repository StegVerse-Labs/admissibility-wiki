# Consequence Standing

Consequence standing is the condition under which a transition's result has governance legitimacy when it binds.

A consequence may be technically produced without having standing.

## Why Standing Can Fail

Standing can fail when:

- the actor lacks authority;
- the policy reference no longer applies;
- evidence is missing or stale;
- context has drifted;
- review is required but absent;
- the boundary of the system does not support the claimed action.

## Relationship to Admissibility

Admissibility is the decision posture.

Consequence standing is what that posture protects.

A transition governance system should refuse, reject, or escalate when consequence standing cannot be established.
