# GLM External Framework Crosswalk

## Status

```text
Relationship type: external framework crosswalk
Canonical StegVerse formalism source: Admissible-Existence
External framework role: pre-admissibility boundary declaration
Wiki role: convergence, mapping, and relationship review
```

## Definition

GLM is treated in this wiki as an external framework for machine-readable boundary declarations.

Its primary relationship to the Admissibility Wiki is that it may describe the declared boundaries, claims, non-claims, composition scope, and interpretation frame that exist before a StegVerse or Admissible-Existence admissibility question is evaluated.

## Why It Matters

GLM can help a non-StegVerse ecosystem declare:

```text
what is being claimed
what is not being claimed
which boundary applies
which composition is in scope
which interpretation frame should be used
```

That can provide a useful step before transition admissibility review.

## Relationship To Admissibility

GLM does not replace admissibility review.

A useful shorthand is:

```text
GLM asks: What boundary is declared before interpretation?
Admissibility asks: May this transition bind consequence at commit time?
EVIDE asks: What evidence remains after the event?
```

## Candidate Relationship Types

GLM relationship candidates may be classified as:

```text
precondition
adjacent
strongly_correlated
overlapping
broader
narrower
unresolved
```

GLM should not be automatically classified as synonymous with any Admissible-Existence formalism unless a formal source supports that relationship and a decision record accepts it.

## Source Boundary

GLM is an external framework. It is not the canonical source for Admissible-Existence formalisms.

The wiki may link to GLM artifacts, compare GLM vocabulary to AE/StegVerse vocabulary, and record relationship candidates. It must not treat GLM as defining StegVerse or Admissible-Existence concepts.

## Audience Expansion

Adding GLM helps the wiki support readers from non-StegVerse ecosystems who already use boundary-declaration, manifest, schema, model-card, system-card, policy, or composition-declaration approaches.

For those readers, GLM can act as a bridge into admissibility terminology without requiring them to start inside StegVerse repository structure.

## Non-Claims

```text
GLM is not presented here as a StegVerse canonical formalism.
GLM does not prove transition admissibility.
GLM does not grant execution authority.
A GLM declaration may support review, but it does not replace commit-time validity.
The wiki relationship record is not acceptance of equivalence.
```
