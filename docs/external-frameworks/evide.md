## Definition

EVIDE is treated in this wiki as an external framework for preserving or organizing evidence that remains after an event, transition, decision, or execution attempt.

Its primary relationship to the Admissibility Wiki is reconstructability: what evidence remains after the fact, whether a reviewer can inspect it, and whether the evidentiary path can explain what occurred without relying only on narrative descriptions.

## Why It Matters

EVIDE can help a non-StegVerse ecosystem describe:

```text
what evidence remains
which receipt or artifact records the event
which reconstruction path is available
which dispute path can inspect the evidence
which post-event claims are supported or unsupported
```

That can provide a useful step after a transition or attempted transition has occurred.

## Relationship To Admissibility

EVIDE does not replace admissibility review.

A useful shorthand is:

```text
GLM asks: What boundary is declared before interpretation?
Admissibility asks: May this transition bind consequence at commit time?
EVIDE asks: What evidence remains after the event?
```

## Source Boundary

EVIDE is an external framework. It is not the canonical source for Admissible-Existence formalisms.

The wiki may link to EVIDE artifacts, compare EVIDE vocabulary to AE/StegVerse vocabulary, and record relationship candidates. It must not treat EVIDE as defining StegVerse or Admissible-Existence concepts.

## Audience Expansion

Adding EVIDE helps the wiki support readers from non-StegVerse ecosystems focused on audit records, evidence packets, incident review, provenance, traceability, dispute resolution, and post-event reconstruction.

For those readers, EVIDE can act as a bridge into receipt-bound execution and reconstructability terminology without requiring them to start inside StegVerse repository structure.
