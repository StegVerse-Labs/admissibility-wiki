# Decision Continuity

## Status

Canonical source repository: `Admissible-Existence/DC`

Wiki state: watching

Detail status: initial public-safe canonical detail page

## Definition

Decision Continuity is a canonical formalism for evaluating whether decision standing persists across review, handoff, state change, publication, or execution boundaries.

It supports inspection of whether a later action can still rely on a prior decision record, or whether the decision must be re-bound against current authority, evidence, policy, and boundary state.

## Source Boundary

Admissible-Existence defines the formalism. Publisher publishes papers and public exposition. The Admissibility Wiki mirrors, relates, crosswalks, and discovers relationships.

The wiki is not the source authority for this formalism.

## Scope

Decision Continuity applies where a decision record must preserve standing across time, systems, actors, receipts, or state transitions.

## Purpose

The purpose of Decision Continuity is to prevent systems from treating an old decision artifact as current authority when the conditions that made it admissible may have changed.

## Core Constructs

| Construct | Role |
|---|---|
| Decision record | Record of a decision, review, or authority posture. |
| Decision standing | Whether the decision can still support consequence. |
| Decision continuity | Whether standing survives across transition boundaries. |
| Rebinding | A renewed check against current state. |
| Supersession | Replacement of an older decision record by a newer one. |
| Receipt | Record supporting reconstruction of the decision path. |
