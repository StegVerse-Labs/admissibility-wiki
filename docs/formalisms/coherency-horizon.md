# Coherency Horizon

## Status

Canonical source repository: `Admissible-Existence/CHF`

Wiki state: watching

Detail status: initial public-safe canonical detail page

## Definition

Coherency Horizon is the CHF formalism for identifying the boundary beyond which no additional information can be obtained from within a stabilized coherent state, even though existence may continue inside that boundary.

It separates existence from knowability, reconstructability, and governable information access.

## Source Boundary

Admissible-Existence defines the formalism. Publisher publishes papers and public exposition. The Admissibility Wiki mirrors, relates, crosswalks, and discovers relationships.

The wiki is not the source authority for this formalism.

## Correction Note

CHF is corrected to mean Coherency Horizon.

Any prior use of CHF for Continuity Handoff Formalism is treated as an abbreviation collision. Handoff continuity may remain a related concept, but it should not occupy the CHF abbreviation when Coherency Horizon is being formalized.

## Scope

Coherency Horizon applies where an entity, state, system, record, or transition may still exist, yet no further retrievable, reconstructable, or governable information can be obtained from within the stabilized coherent boundary.

## Purpose

The purpose of Coherency Horizon is to prevent admissibility from relying on existence alone.

A state can continue to exist inside a coherent boundary. That does not mean the state can still produce usable information for governance, reconstruction, proof, or commit-time standing.

## Core Distinction

```text
%Existence asks: does the entity, state, or relation still exist?
Coherency Horizon asks: can information about it still be obtained or reconstructed?
Commit-Time Admissibility asks: can a transition bind consequence now?
```

## Core Constructs

| Construct | Role |
|---|---|
| Coherent state | A stabilized state whose internal relations have settled enough to be treated as coherent. |
| Coherent boundary | The boundary within which the stabilized coherent state persists. |
| Coherency horizon | The boundary beyond which no additional information can be obtained from within the stabilized coherent state. |
| %Existence | The existence posture of the entity, state, or relation inside the boundary. |
| Information access | Whether usable information can still be retrieved, observed, reconstructed, or emitted. |
| Reconstruction limit | The point beyond which receipts, evidence, memory, or observation cannot recover additional standing. |
| Governance confidence | Confidence that a decision can still be made from available reconstructable information. |
| Fail-closed trigger | The conservative result when existence may remain but knowability or reconstructability has crossed the horizon. |

## Relationship to Relational Transition Geometry

Coherency Horizon sits inside Relational Transition Geometry as the knowability boundary.

Relational Transition Geometry describes the structure of a transition relationship. Coherency Horizon determines whether the information needed to govern or reconstruct that relationship remains available.

## Relationship to the Transition Table

The Transition Table should not classify a transition as admissible merely because the target state still exists.

If the relevant evidence, authority, context, memory, or recoverability information has crossed the Coherency Horizon, the table posture should reflect loss of reconstructable standing, degraded governance confidence, or fail-closed behavior.

## Relationship to Commit-Time Admissibility

Commit-Time Admissibility depends on reconstructable standing.

If a proposed transition is outside the Coherency Horizon, the system cannot safely infer that authority, evidence, context, or recoverability remains valid. The correct conservative posture is DENY, ESCALATE, or FAIL-CLOSED according to the governing table cell.
