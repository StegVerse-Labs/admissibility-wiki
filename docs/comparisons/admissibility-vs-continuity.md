# Admissibility vs Continuity

Continuity asks whether a path, identity, or process remains connected across time.

Admissibility asks whether a transition may bind under the authority, policy, evidence, and context state present at commit time.

## Difference

A system can preserve continuity while losing admissibility.

For example, an agent may remain technically continuous while the policy basis that authorized its next action has changed.

## Practical Rule

Continuity can help prove that the same path continued.

Admissibility must still prove that the next consequence had standing.
